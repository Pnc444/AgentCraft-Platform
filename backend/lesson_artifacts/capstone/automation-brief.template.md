# The Automation Brief — four questions, four lines

A brief is finished when each question has one honest line. Same standard as the Module 4 blueprint: honest sentences, not polished ones.

---

## Worked example: the Monday audit

- **Trigger:** A schedule — every Monday at 9:00.
- **Steps:** Run `openclaw security audit`; collect the findings into a checklist.
- **Review point:** The checklist comes to me; I decide what changes. Nothing changes itself.
- **Receipt:** The audit report, saved with a date.

Notice every answer is one line. That is the target amount of detail.

---

## Your brief

- **Trigger:** (what starts it — a schedule, an arriving message, or a webhook?)

- **Steps:** (a list you could draw — not "the AI handles it")

- **Review point:** (where does a person approve before anything irreversible?)

- **Receipt:** (what written record proves the run happened?)

---

Design rule worth keeping forever: **no receipt, no automation** — not as a punishment, but because you deserve to be able to look things up rather than wonder.
