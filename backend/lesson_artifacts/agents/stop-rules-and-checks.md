# Checks and Stop Rules — one card

## Checks: the system carries the doubt

A check is a moment where the agent examines its own work before believing it.

- **Judging inputs** — "Is this article actually about sleep and memory?" (Juno, before keeping a source)
- **Verifying outputs** — re-reading a summary against its source before delivering it

When the agent does the doubting, you don't have to.

## Stop rules: the finish line is drawn before the race

1. **Goal met** — "three good summaries delivered" → stop.
2. **Limit hit** — "at most ten searches" or "at most five minutes" → stop and report how far it got.
3. **Ask a human** — anything ambiguous or risky → stop and hand it back.

**Limit hit** is the trust-builder: an agent with a limit always comes back, even when the errand goes sideways. It can never silently run away with a task.

---

A finish line written in advance is what makes finishing real. (That is also why every lesson in this course ends with a "done means done" list.)
