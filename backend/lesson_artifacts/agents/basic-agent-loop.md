# The Loop: five steps, one card

1. **Understand** the goal.
2. **Plan** the next step.
3. **Act**: use a tool.
4. **Look** at what happened.
5. **Decide**: continue, adjust, ask a human, or stop.

Steps 4 and 5 are where a human fits: the decide step can always hand work back to a person when something is risky or ambiguous.

## Juno's errand, condensed

> Goal: three good articles on sleep and memory, summarized.
> Search: ten results. **Look:** one solid study, one mattress ad, one paywall.
> **Decide:** keep the study, skip the rest, continue.
> Narrower search: two more solid articles. That makes three.
> Summarize, deliver, **stop.**

The paywall was not a crisis. The loop absorbed it. In a loop, a failed step is information for the next decide, not an emergency. And the first plan was revised mid-errand, which is the loop working exactly as designed.
