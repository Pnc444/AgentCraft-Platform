# Answerer, Checklist, or Agent?

| | Answerer (chatbot) | Checklist (workflow) | Agent |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| What it does | Replies to what you ask | Runs the same fixed steps every time | Chooses its next step from what it just found |
| Best at | Questions with known answers | Repeatable tasks with stable steps | Tasks where findings change the plan |
| Its limit | Cannot act on the world | Cannot change course midstream | Costs more; needs checks and stop rules to trust |
| How to spot one | Question in, answer out — nothing else happens | You could write every step down before it runs | The second step depends on how the first one went |

**The sorting question:** *Can I write down all the steps in advance?*
Yes → checklist. No, because steps depend on findings → agent. Just a reply needed → answerer.

Choosing the simplest shape that solves the job is good engineering, not timidity.
