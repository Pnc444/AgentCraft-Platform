# What Each Model is Good At

While most of the models in the series below can hold reasonable chat conversations and provide human-like feedback, they still have different merits.

### Hermes
- As open-weight models intended to be "neutral," these models have intentionally weaker guardrails, and can be run on local hardware. They may be useful for testing redteaming scenarios, or seeing how "unrestricted" language models act.

### Claude
- Because Anthropic (Claude) and OpenAI (GPT-X series) both offer their models with agentic capabilities(the ability to tell software to run specific code, and process the result) and third-party integrations, it is less helpful to compare their ecosystems. Both models offer similar features, but the Claude models are most well-known for the use of a "constitution" document in post-training. This approach (which has changed over time) was partially inspired by the UN Declaration of Human Rights, and is unique to Anthropic.
- Users of Claude models report the models respond to writing prompts more "organically" and with less boilerplate than OpenAI's models do.

### Gemini
- While other models now support image and video, Gemini's earliest models were famous for being multimodal. Instead of converting non-text input into a format that could be understood by text-based encoders, Google created a platform that could understand text, image, and video data natively.
